After the presentation, the team reviewed and updated the slide content to finalize the report materials.

During the presentation, the team covered two algorithms:
- Dijkstra
- Floyd (Floyd-Warshall)

Due to time constraints, A* was not included in the live presentation. Afterward, the team fully added the A* algorithm to the slide deck and summary materials.

## 2) Updated Content

### 2.1 Content Kept Unchanged
- Overview of the shortest path problem.
- Dijkstra section and examples.
- Floyd section and examples.

### 2.2 Content Added
- Added a dedicated section for the A* algorithm, including:
	- The concept and idea of the evaluation function $f(n) = g(n) + h(n)$.
	- Explanation of the meaning of $g(n)$, $h(n)$, and heuristic selection criteria.
	- Basic workflow of A*.
	- A step-by-step illustrative example.
	- Evaluation of advantages, limitations, and usage conditions.

### 2.3 Content Revised
- Updated the comparative section to include A* alongside Dijkstra and Floyd.
- Revised the conclusion to clearly classify usage scenarios:
	- Dijkstra: suitable for graphs with non-negative edge weights when exact results are required.
	- Floyd: suitable for all-pairs shortest paths problems.
	- A*: suitable for pathfinding with a clear destination and a good heuristic.
